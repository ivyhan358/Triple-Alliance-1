History Of National Kandawgyi Garden

The Botanical Garden was founded in 1915 by forest researcher Mr. Alex Rogers, who planned and established it as an international garden. In 1917, Mr. Otway Wheeler Cuffe and his wife, Charlotte Wheeler Cuffe, assisted with ideas, formation, and landscaping inspired by London’s Kew Gardens. Originally 150 acres, it was expanded to 240 acres and designated the National Botanical Garden on December 1st, 1924, under the Ministry of Forestry. After major renovations in 2000, the garden gained improved landscaping and scenic views. Later, a 215-foot-tall tower with a 50-foot diameter was built in the National Kandawgyi Gardens in 2002 and opened in 2003, featuring 12 levels, including 10 viewing floors and a Function Hall.

Flower Festival

The most renowned event of the garden is flower festival which takes place annually during December and January. It aims at in order to promote garden's activity and to enhance the industry of flower production in Myanmar. Obviously, the festival contributes appreciable advantages to local business and tourism development as well. Usually, more than 600,000 individuals of visitors are coming for this December Event.
